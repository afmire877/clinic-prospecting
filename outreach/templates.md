# Outreach Templates: Discovery-Phase Conversations

---

## Your Positioning (Internal — Not for Prospects)

You are **not selling anything**. You are genuinely researching the aesthetics industry to understand what clinic owners actually struggle with before you build or offer anything. Every conversation is valuable data regardless of whether it leads to business.

This framing is not a trick — it is strategically correct when you don't have a value proposition yet. You will learn more from honest curiosity than from a premature pitch.

**Rules:**
- Never mention a product or service
- Never hint at what you might sell
- Be specific about what you noticed about their business
- Keep messages short — busy clinic owners won't read essays
- If they ask "so what do you do?" — be honest: "I'm a developer exploring the aesthetics space. Right now I'm just trying to understand the real challenges before I build anything."

## 1. Cold Email

**When to use:** When a clinic has a publicly listed email on their website or Google listing.

### Subject Line Options (Test Different Ones)

- Quick question about running a clinic in [area]
- Researching the aesthetics industry — would love your perspective
- How do [area] clinic owners handle [specific pain point]?
- Curious about something I noticed on your website

### Email Body

```
Hi [Owner first name],

I came across [clinic name] on [where you found them — Google, Instagram, etc.]
and [one specific, genuine compliment — e.g., "your before/after results for
lip fillers are really impressive" or "your Google reviews are glowing,
especially the ones mentioning how welcoming the clinic feels"].

I'm based in Barking and I'm researching what it's really like to run an
independent aesthetics clinic in Essex. I keep hearing that [mention one
pain point — e.g., "no-shows are a nightmare" or "marketing takes up more
time than actual treatments"] — is that true for you?

I'd love to buy you a coffee and ask 3–4 questions. No pitch, no agenda —
I'm genuinely trying to understand the industry before I do anything in it.

Would a 15-minute phone call work, or I'm happy to pop into the clinic
if that's easier?

[Your name]
[Your phone number]
```

**Length:** ~120 words. Do not exceed 150.

**Key:** The compliment and the pain point reference must be specific to THIS clinic. Generic = deleted.

## 2. Instagram DM

### Pre-DM Warmup (Critical — Do Not Skip)

Before sending any DM, spend 2–3 days engaging with their content:

1. **Day 1:** Like 2–3 of their recent posts. Leave a genuine comment on one (not "great post" — something specific like "that jawline contouring result is incredible, was that filler or threads?")
2. **Day 2:** Like another post. Comment on a reel or story if they post one.
3. **Day 3:** Now send the DM. They'll have seen your name in their notifications by now.

### Cold DM (After Warmup)

```
Hey [first name]! Your [specific treatment] results are
genuinely impressive — especially [reference a specific post].

Random question — I've been researching what it takes to
run an independent clinic in [area]. What's been your
biggest surprise about the business side of things?

No agenda, just curious. I'm based locally in Barking.
```

**Rules:**
- Maximum 2–3 sentences
- Ask ONE question, not five
- Match their tone — if their posts are casual, be casual
- Never open with "Hey, I have a business opportunity" or anything that sounds like MLM

### Warm DM (After They've Engaged Back)

If they've replied to a comment or liked your response:

```
Thanks for the reply on that post! I've actually been
chatting with a few clinic owners in [area] about
what the day-to-day is really like.

Would you be up for a quick 10-minute call sometime?
I'd love to pick your brain — no pitch, just trying
to learn from people actually doing it.
```

## 3. Walk-In / In-Person

**Best for:** Clinics close to Barking (Romford, Hornchurch, Stratford, Canary Wharf). This is your strongest channel — face-to-face builds trust fastest.

### Timing

- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday
- **Best times:** 10am–12pm or 2pm–4pm (quieter periods)
- **Avoid:** Monday mornings, Friday afternoons, Saturdays (their busiest times)

### What to Wear

Smart-casual. Not a suit (too corporate), not joggers (not credible). Clean jeans, nice shoes, plain shirt/jumper.

### Have Ready

- A simple card or piece of paper with: your name, phone number, and "Researching the aesthetics industry"
- Nothing else — no brochure, no flyer, no laptop

### The Script

**Walk in, look around, approach reception:**

> "Hi! I was passing by and I've actually been meaning to check this place out. The clinic looks lovely."

*Let them respond. If they offer information about treatments, listen.*

**Transition:**

> "I'm actually doing a bit of research on aesthetics clinics in the area — I'm based locally in Barking. Is the owner around by any chance? I'd love to ask them a quick question about running the business."

**If the owner is there:**

> "Hi [name], thanks for taking a minute. I've been chatting with clinic owners in [area] trying to understand what the biggest challenges are. I keep hearing [one pain point] — is that something you deal with?"

*Then listen. Let them talk. Ask follow-up questions. Don't steer the conversation toward anything you could sell.*

**If the owner isn't there:**

> "No worries at all. Could you pass this along?" [Hand over your card/note] "I'd love to chat with [owner name] for 10 minutes about their experience. Completely informal — just research."

### Exit

Don't overstay. 5–7 minutes maximum unless they clearly want to keep talking.

> "I really appreciate your time — this is genuinely helpful. If you ever want to chat more, my number's on that card."

### Bonus Move

If the clinic offers a treatment you'd actually consider getting (e.g., a facial, consultation), book it. Becoming a genuine client builds real relationship and gives you firsthand experience of their booking flow, client communication, and service quality. This is also legitimate market research.

## 4. Follow-Up Sequence

### Rules

- Never follow up more than 3 times without a response
- Each follow-up must add value — never send "just checking in" or "bumping this"
- If no response after 3 touches, move them to a "revisit in 3 months" column in the lead tracker
- Space follow-ups so you don't feel pushy

### Touch 1 — Same Day or Next Day

**Channel:** Same channel as initial outreach (email, DM, etc.)

**Template:**

```
Hi [name], just wanted to say thanks for [chatting with me /
taking the time to reply / reading my message].

The point you made about [reference one specific thing they
said — or if they haven't replied yet, reference something
you noticed about their business] really stuck with me.

Hope you have a great rest of the week.
```

**Purpose:** Show you're a real person who listens. No ask.

### Touch 2 — 3 to 5 Days Later

**Template:**

```
Hi [name], following up on what you mentioned about [pain point
they shared — or a common pain point if they haven't responded].

I came across [share something genuinely valuable — an article,
a stat, a tool, an insight from another conversation (anonymised)].
Thought it might be useful for you:

[Link or brief summary]

No response needed — just thought of you.
```

**Purpose:** Give before you ask. Demonstrate you're thinking about their problems.

**Examples of things to share:**
- A stat about no-show reduction with automated reminders
- An article about upcoming aesthetics regulation
- A tip from another clinic owner (anonymised) about a booking system they like
- A before/after marketing example from a clinic in a different area

### Touch 3 — 7 to 10 Days Later

**Template:**

```
Hi [name], last message from me — don't want to be a pest!

I've now spoken to [X] clinic owners in [area] and I'm starting
to see some clear patterns about what makes running an independent
clinic genuinely hard.

Would you be interested in me sharing what I've found? Not selling
anything — just thought you might find it useful to see how your
experience compares to others in the area.

Either way, wishing you all the best with [clinic name].
```

**Purpose:** This is the conversion message. You're offering something valuable (aggregated insights) in exchange for a conversation. This is genuine — by this point you should actually have insights to share.

## 5. Discovery Call Questions

If you get a call booked, here's a loose structure. **Do not read these out like a script.** Have a natural conversation — these are guardrails, not a checklist.

### Opening (2 minutes)

- Thank them for their time
- Reiterate: "I'm not selling anything — I'm just trying to understand the industry"
- Ask: "How did you get into aesthetics?"

### Core Questions (8–10 minutes)

Pick 3–4 of these based on the conversation flow:

1. "What does a typical week look like for you?"
2. "What's the thing that takes up the most time that isn't actually doing treatments?"
3. "How do people find out about you and book?"
4. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about running the business, what would it be?"
5. "What software or tools do you use day to day? Are you happy with them?"
6. "How do you handle no-shows?"
7. "Do you track which clients haven't been back in a while?"
8. "Have you ever worked with a marketing agency? How was that experience?"

### Close (2 minutes)

- Summarise what you heard: "So it sounds like [X] and [Y] are the big things for you"
- Ask: "Is there anyone else in the area you think I should talk to?" (Referrals)
- Thank them genuinely

### After the Call

- Immediately log the conversation in `research/target-market.md` conversation log
- Update `lead-tracker.csv` with pain points and notes
- Send Touch 1 follow-up (thank you message)
