## Pre-Session Setup (10 minutes)

1. Open `prospecting/lead-tracker.csv` in Google Sheets or Excel
2. Open these tabs in your browser:
   - [Google Maps](https://www.google.co.uk/maps)
   - Instagram (logged in)
   - [Save Face](https://www.saveface.co.uk/)
   - [Glowday](https://www.glowday.com/)
   - [Treatwell](https://www.treatwell.co.uk/)
3. Set a timer for 2.5 hours (hard stop — don't let research expand indefinitely)
4. Pick which geographic area to focus on this session (rotate through areas):
   - Session A: Brentwood / Hornchurch
   - Session B: Chelmsford / Romford
   - Session C: Canary Wharf / Stratford
   - Session D: Tier 2 areas (once Tier 1 is covered)
5. Check lead tracker for any follow-ups due today

## Phase 1: Discovery — Finding Clinics (60–90 minutes)

The goal is to build a raw list of 8–12 clinics. Don't qualify yet — just find them.

### Method A: Google Maps Sweep

Search these terms for your chosen area (replace `[area]` with today's focus):

```
aesthetics clinic [area]
med spa [area]
anti wrinkle injections [area]
dermal fillers [area]
skin clinic [area]
lip fillers [area]
cosmetic clinic [area]
beauty clinic botox [area]
```

**For each clinic found, note:**
- Name
- Address
- Google rating and review count
- Whether they have a website linked
- Booking method visible (online button? phone number? "DM to book"?)

**Pro tip:** Also check Google pages 2–3 for `aesthetics clinic [area]` — these are clinics with weaker SEO, which signals they might need help.

### Method B: Instagram Discovery

Search by location tags:
- Brentwood, Chelmsford, Romford, Hornchurch, Canary Wharf, Stratford

Search by hashtags:
- `#essexaesthetics` `#chelmsfordaesthetics` `#brentwoodaesthetics`
- `#essexbotox` `#essexfillers` `#essexskincare`
- `#romfordaesthetics` `#hornchurchaesthetics`
- `#canarywharf beauty` `#stratfordaesthetics`
- `#aestheticsclinik` `#aestheticsnurse` `#lipfillersessex`
- `#botoxessex` `#skinclinicessex`

**What to note:**
- Handle and follower count
- Posting frequency (scroll to see gaps)
- Engagement rate (likes ÷ followers — under 1% = weak)
- Content quality and variety
- Whether they link to a booking system in bio

**Also:** Look at who's following and followed by clinics you've already found — this reveals more clinics in the same area.

### Method C: Directory Mining

**Save Face (saveface.co.uk):**
- Filter by location
- Note which clinics ARE registered (credible) and which AREN'T (opportunity signal — they may not know about it or haven't prioritised compliance)

**Glowday (glowday.com):**
- Search by area
- Note profile completeness — thin profiles signal less digital sophistication

**Treatwell (treatwell.co.uk):**
- Search by area for aesthetics/skin treatments
- Clinics listed here may be struggling for visibility (Treatwell takes a commission, so clinics using it likely don't have strong organic acquisition)

## Phase 2: Qualification — Scoring Leads (30–45 minutes)

Go through your raw list and score each clinic using this rubric. Everything can be checked from your desk in under 2 minutes per clinic.

### Scoring Rubric

| Signal | Points | How to Check |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| No website or very basic template site | +3 | Google search their name |
| No online booking (call/DM/WhatsApp only) | +3 | Check website + Instagram bio |
| Fewer than 20 Google reviews | +2 | Google Maps |
| No Google reviews in the last 3 months | +2 | Google Maps → sort by newest |
| Instagram posting gaps (>2 weeks between posts) | +2 | Scroll their Instagram feed |
| Low engagement rate (<1% of followers liking posts) | +1 | Quick mental math on recent posts |
| Not on Save Face register | +1 | Search saveface.co.uk |
| Using Fresha or Booksy free tier | +1 | Click their booking link, check URL |
| Owner-operator visible (single practitioner) | +2 | Website "About" page |
| Located in Tier 1 geography | +1 | Address |

**Maximum possible score: 18**

### Score Interpretation

| Score | Priority | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| 8+ | High | Reach out this week |
| 5–7 | Medium | Add to outreach queue, contact within 2 weeks |
| 3–4 | Low | Monitor — may not need help or may be too early stage |
| Below 3 | Skip | Too sophisticated or too small — move on |

Log the score in `lead-tracker.csv` for each clinic.

## Phase 3: Outreach Prep — Personalising Messages (30–45 minutes)

Take your top **3–5 leads** from this session (highest scores) and prepare personalised outreach.

### For each lead:

1. **Deep research (5 minutes per clinic):**
   - Read their "About" page — who is the owner? What's their background?
   - Check their last 9–12 Instagram posts — what are they proud of? What treatments do they push?
   - Read their Google reviews — what do clients praise? What do they complain about?
   - Try to book online — how friction-heavy is the process?

2. **Find your hooks:**
   - One genuine compliment (specific, not generic — reference an actual post, review, or detail)
   - One observation about their business (not critical, just curious — e.g., "I noticed you don't seem to have online booking")

3. **Choose the channel:**
   - Email available on website? → Cold email
   - No email but active on Instagram? → DM (but engage with 2–3 posts first over the next few days)
   - Very close to Barking (Romford, Hornchurch, Stratford)? → Consider walk-in

4. **Draft the message** using templates from `outreach/templates.md`

5. **Log everything** in `lead-tracker.csv` — channel, date, personalisation notes

### Sending Cadence

Don't send all outreach in one burst. Spread across the week:
- **Session day:** Draft messages
- **Next 1–2 days:** Send 1–2 per day (so responses are manageable and you can reply promptly)

## Post-Session Review (10 minutes)

1. Update `lead-tracker.csv` with all new leads found today
2. Note any follow-ups scheduled for specific dates
3. Write 2–3 bullet points of patterns you noticed:
   - Were most clinics in this area on Fresha?
   - Did any area have surprisingly good/bad digital presence?
   - Any new search terms or hashtags that worked well?
4. Decide which area to focus on next session

---

## Weekly Review (30 minutes, end of week)

Run through these questions every Friday:

- **Numbers:** How many leads found / qualified / contacted this week?
- **Response rate:** What percentage replied? Which channel performed best?
- **Conversations:** Any discovery calls booked or completed?
- **Pain points:** What themes came up in conversations? (Update `research/target-market.md`)
- **Adjustments:** Should I change my qualification criteria? My outreach approach? My target area?

---

## Monthly Review (1 hour, last day of month)

Deeper strategic review:

- **Consolidated learnings:** What have you learned from all conversations this month?
- **Pain point ranking:** Which problems came up most often? Update `research/target-market.md` pain point rankings.
- **Value proposition hypothesis:** Based on what you've heard, what could you offer that they'd pay for? Write 1–2 sentences.
- **Geographic performance:** Which tiers/areas are most responsive? Should you shift focus?
- **Process improvements:** What's working in your prospecting system? What's wasting time?
- **Next month focus:** Set specific targets for leads, outreach, and calls.

| `owner_name` | Owner/lead practitioner (if found) |

| `website` | Full URL |
| `instagram_handle` | @handle |
| `instagram_followers` | Approximate follower count |
| `google_rating` | Star rating (e.g., 4.8) |

| `last_review_date` | Date of most recent review |
| `booking_method` | online / phone / dm / whatsapp / mixed |

| `outreach_channel` | email / instagram / in-person / phone |
| `outreach_date` | Date first outreach sent |
| `outreach_status` | not_contacted / contacted / responded / call_booked / call_done / no_response |
| `follow_up_1_date` | Date of first follow-up |
| `follow_up_2_date` | Date of second follow-up |
| `follow_up_3_date` | Date of third follow-up |
| `discovery_call_date` | Date of discovery call if booked |
| `key_pain_points` | Free text — what they said matters most |
| `notes` | Anything else |
| `last_updated` | Date this row was last modified |
